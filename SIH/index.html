Problem Context

Understanding the mental health crisis in higher education

College Students at Risk

Mental health issues among college students have significantly increased in recent years, including anxiety, depression, burnout, sleep disorders, academic stress, and social isolation.

Rural & Semi-Urban Gap

There is a major gap in the availability, accessibility, and stigma-free delivery of mental health support in most higher education institutions, especially in rural and semi-urban colleges.

Critical Need

The current system lacks structured, scalable, and stigma-free psychological intervention mechanisms that can reach students when they need help most.

Problems Faced

Key challenges in current mental health support systems

01

Absence of Structured System

No structured, scalable, and stigma-free psychological intervention system exists in most institutions.

02

Lack of Early Detection

No early detection and preventive mental health tools are available to identify at-risk students.

03

Under-utilization of Services

College counseling centres are under-utilized due to fear of judgment or lack of awareness among students.

04

No Centralized Monitoring

No centralized mental health monitoring or data-driven policy framework exists within institutions.

System Features

Five core capabilities of the digital intervention system

AI-guided First-Aid Support

Interactive chat box that offers coping strategies and refers students to professionals when needed. Provides immediate emotional support and crisis intervention.

24/7 Available Crisis Support Professional Referral

Confidential Booking System

Secure appointment booking for on-campus counselors or mental health helplines. Ensures privacy and removes barriers to seeking help.

Privacy Protected Easy Scheduling Reminder System

Psychoeducational Resource Hub

Comprehensive library of videos, relaxation audio, and mental wellness guides available in regional languages for cultural relevance.

Multilingual Cultural Context Self-Help Tools

Peer Support Platform

Moderated peer-to-peer support forum with trained student volunteers. Creates a safe space for shared experiences and mutual support.

Peer Connection Moderated Trained Volunteers

Admin Dashboard

Anonymous data analytics for authorities to recognize trends and plan interventions. Enables data-driven mental health policy decisions.

Anonymous Data Trend Analysis Policy Support
